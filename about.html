My Journey: From a Village

I grew up in a remote village where basic infrastructure like internet, electricity, and even proper water supply. Despite these challenges, my curiosity to understand how things work never wavered. I didn’t have access to computers, but I was driven by a fascination for technology. When the pandemic hit, we finally got internet, and I realized the power of connectivity and knowledge.

Pre Internet Life & Early Engineering Mindset

Even before the internet arrived in my life (which was in pandemic in summer of 2019), I was always finding creative ways to solve problems. I once tried to root my father's smartphone, hoping to boost its RAM to play PC games. It failed, but it taught me about hacking and system architecture.

At 10, I was fascinated by a glue gun but couldn’t afford one, so I built my own using materials like a heating coil from a mosquito repellent. This DIY approach was a theme throughout my childhood. Like, I once experimented with passing AC current through water to heat it, which ended in a pretty big explosion and smoke-filled house! My parents weren't thrilled and the greater surprise was when electrician asked my dad if someone died of that incident.

I also built various tools like a foam cutter from nichrome wire, a soldering iron,an Arduino-based alarm system and a human following rover inspired by a space tech movie (but couldn't really finish it because I was out of budget). I’m always challenging myself, whether it’s through building new devices or solving complex problems with limited resources. This foundation inspired me to pursue larger and more impactful projects.
